# Chronicle Manifesto

## Preamble

Blockchains preserve transactions, but ecosystems are built by people.

A network can remember balances while forgetting the contributors who wrote the first guides, operated the early infrastructure, reviewed the first proposals, translated the first documents, explained the first errors, and carried knowledge from one generation of participants to the next.

Chronicle / Legacy Protocol begins from a simple premise: decentralized ecosystems need memory.

## 1. Contribution Should Not Disappear

Human contribution is often scattered across platforms that were not designed for long-term memory. Pull requests, governance posts, forum comments, chat explanations, infrastructure notes, and educational materials can disappear from collective awareness even when they shaped the ecosystem.

Chronicle exists to preserve contribution as structured memory.

## 2. Memory Comes Before Reward

A system that begins with rewards may invite farming. A system that begins with memory asks a different question: what happened, who contributed, what evidence exists, and why did it matter?

Chronicle is not a farming mechanism. It is a memory protocol.

## 3. Reputation Must Have Context

Reputation without context becomes a score. Scores can be gamed, bought, misunderstood, or weaponized. Real contribution is category-specific, evidence-dependent, and historically situated.

Chronicle treats reputation as a map of contribution history, not as a single number.

## 4. Knowledge Should Be Inherited

Every ecosystem contains lessons that should not need to be relearned from the beginning. Technical constraints, governance trade-offs, community norms, infrastructure failures, and documentation decisions all form part of an ecosystem's inheritance.

Chronicle seeks to preserve this inheritance for future contributors.

## 5. AI Should Guide, Not Govern

AI can help people navigate archives, summarize history, and understand prior decisions. But AI should not become the source of truth. It must remain source-linked, uncertainty-aware, and subordinate to human judgment and governance.

Chronicle imagines AI as a mentor layer, not an oracle.

## 6. Legacy Should Stay Open

Legacy must not become permanent hierarchy. Early contributors should be remembered, but future contributors must not be locked out by the past. A healthy memory layer honors history while keeping participation open.

Chronicle preserves legacy without freezing power.

## 7. Ecosystems Need Continuity

Decentralized systems often focus on launch moments, incentives, and technical milestones. But long-term ecosystems also need continuity: memory, trust, knowledge transfer, contributor recognition, and historical accountability.

Chronicle is a proposal for that continuity.

## Closing Statement

Chronicle / Legacy Protocol is an invitation to study how decentralized ecosystems can remember their builders, preserve their knowledge, and pass their history forward without turning contribution into speculation.

The goal is not to make memory profitable.

The goal is to make memory durable.
