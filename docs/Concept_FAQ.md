# Concept FAQ

## What is Chronicle / Legacy Protocol?

Chronicle / Legacy Protocol is a conceptual research project for a Protocol Memory Layer. It studies how decentralized ecosystems can preserve contribution history, knowledge, governance context, reputation signals, and long-term ecosystem memory in a structured and reviewable way.

It is not an implementation project at the current stage. The repository focuses on architecture, documentation, research questions, and conceptual clarity.

## What problem does Chronicle solve?

Many blockchain and open-source ecosystems preserve transactions and code history better than they preserve human contribution context. Documentation work, governance reasoning, educational material, infrastructure support, translation, mentorship, and community learning are often scattered across platforms.

Chronicle studies how these records could be preserved as structured memory rather than lost in chats, social feeds, issue threads, or disconnected documents.

## How is Chronicle different from Proof of Contribution?

Chronicle / Legacy Protocol is the broader research framework. It studies protocol memory, knowledge inheritance, governance context, contributor reputation, archival records, and AI-assisted retrieval.

Proof of Contribution is a narrower sub-proposal. It focuses on how contribution claims can be submitted, verified, attested, disputed, and preserved as evidence-aware records.

In simple terms:

| Project | Focus |
|---|---|
| Chronicle / Legacy Protocol | Broad Protocol Memory Layer research |
| QoreChain Proof of Contribution | Contribution verification and attestation research |

## What is a Memory Object?

A Memory Object is a structured and verifiable record of something meaningful within an ecosystem.

It is not the contribution itself. It is the memory record that describes the contribution, decision, knowledge artifact, governance event, or mentorship interaction. A Memory Object may include evidence links, contributor references, context tags, lifecycle status, attestations, and related records.

## What is Knowledge Inheritance?

Knowledge Inheritance is the process of turning archived ecosystem memory into reusable learning context for future participants.

For example, a past documentation improvement, governance decision, or infrastructure lesson may later become part of an onboarding path, contributor handoff note, or AI-assisted explanation. Knowledge Inheritance helps future contributors understand what was learned, why it mattered, and how it should be interpreted.

## What is the Reputation Graph?

The Reputation Graph is a conceptual way to understand how contribution records, attestations, evidence quality, and context-specific reputation may connect over time.

It should not be understood as a single score or leaderboard. Chronicle treats reputation as contextual. A contributor may have meaningful reputation in documentation, governance, infrastructure, research, localization, or education without those contributions being reduced to one universal number.

## What is the AI Mentor Layer?

The AI Mentor Layer is a proposed research component that could help users navigate protocol memory, inherited knowledge, governance history, and contribution context.

The AI Mentor should not act as an authority. It should not approve contributions, assign reputation, resolve governance disputes, or replace human review. Its role is to support source-linked learning, summarize records with uncertainty labels, and help users find relevant documents.

## Is Chronicle a product or a research concept?

Chronicle is currently a research concept and architecture project. It is not a deployed product, protocol, smart contract system, or production application.

The current goal is to define the problem space, architecture, risks, research questions, and documentation structure before any implementation is considered.

## Is Chronicle specific to QoreChain?

Chronicle is inspired by QoreChain ecosystem contribution work, but the concept is broader. It can be studied as a general Protocol Memory Layer model for decentralized ecosystems, open-source communities, governance systems, and contributor networks.

QoreChain-related examples may be used as practical case studies, but Chronicle is not limited to one ecosystem by design.

## Who can contribute?

At the current research stage, useful contributions may include:

- documentation improvements;
- architecture notes;
- research questions;
- threat model improvements;
- related work analysis;
- governance memory examples;
- knowledge inheritance examples;
- contributor reputation safeguards;
- privacy and abuse-resistance considerations.

Contributions should remain research-focused and should avoid promotional claims, reward farming language, unsupported implementation claims, or token/investment framing.

## How could Chronicle evolve in the future?

Chronicle could evolve through several research stages:

1. clearer architecture documents;
2. formal Memory Object schemas;
3. stronger evidence and attestation models;
4. sample decision records and learning paths;
5. privacy and access-control research;
6. AI mentor retrieval experiments;
7. governance review models;
8. prototype tools for indexing and exploring protocol memory.

Any future implementation should follow from research validation, governance review, privacy analysis, and practical ecosystem needs.

## Why does Chronicle avoid reward farming language?

Chronicle is focused on memory, context, verification, and long-term knowledge preservation. If contribution records are treated only as short-term reward claims, the system may encourage spam, artificial activity, or reputation inflation.

The research priority is to preserve meaningful contribution responsibly, not to maximize task completion volume.
