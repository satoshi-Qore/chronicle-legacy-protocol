# 06 - Legacy Protocol

## Overview

Legacy Protocol refers to the long-term preservation layer of Chronicle. It asks how ecosystems can remember contributors, decisions, and knowledge across years rather than only across campaigns, releases, or short-term tasks.

## Meaning of Legacy

Legacy does not mean status worship or permanent hierarchy. It means preserving the record of useful work so that future participants can understand what came before them.

A legacy record may include:

- Major contributions
- Long-term documentation work
- Infrastructure service
- Governance participation
- Research milestones
- Educational contributions
- Security improvements
- Community stewardship

## Time and Memory

Ecosystem memory should account for time. Some contributions matter immediately, while others become important later because they explain why a path was chosen or why a mistake was avoided.

Possible views:

- Recent contribution history
- Lifetime contribution history
- Category-specific contribution history
- Historical decision timelines
- Contributor legacy profiles

## Risks

Legacy systems can create social hierarchy if poorly designed. They must avoid freezing power around early contributors or making new contributors feel excluded.

## Design Position

Legacy should preserve memory, not create permanent status. A healthy protocol should honor past contribution while keeping future participation open.
