# 01 - Proof of Contribution

## Overview

Proof of Contribution is the foundational verification layer of Chronicle / Legacy Protocol. It studies how meaningful human contribution can be documented, reviewed, and preserved without reducing contribution to raw activity counts or short-term reward behavior.

## Concept

A contribution is any action that produces durable value for an ecosystem. This may include code, documentation, infrastructure operation, governance analysis, security review, education, localization, research, onboarding, or community coordination.

Proof of Contribution asks how such work can be represented through structured evidence and attestations.

## Core Questions

- What qualifies as a meaningful ecosystem contribution?
- What evidence should support a contribution claim?
- Who can attest that a contribution was useful or accepted?
- How can contribution records resist spam, duplication, and farming?
- How can small but important contributions be preserved?

## Evidence-Aware Contribution

A contribution record should include context:

| Field | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Contribution type | Describes the kind of work performed |
| Evidence reference | Links to proof such as a PR, issue, article, vote, or archive |
| Attestation status | Shows whether the contribution is claimed, reviewed, accepted, or disputed |
| Contributor identity | Connects work to a person, address, or pseudonymous profile |
| Scope and limitation | Explains what the record proves and what it does not prove |

## Design Position

Proof of Contribution should not become a farming layer. It should prioritize evidence quality, reviewer context, contribution usefulness, and long-term memory.
