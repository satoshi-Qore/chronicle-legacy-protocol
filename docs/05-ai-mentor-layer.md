# 05 - AI Mentor Layer

## Overview

The AI Mentor Layer is a proposed assistive layer that helps transmit ecosystem knowledge to new and existing contributors. It should not replace human governance, maintainer judgment, or contributor agency. Its purpose is to make archived knowledge easier to understand and apply.

## Role of AI

AI may support:

- Explaining historical decisions
- Summarizing governance discussions
- Helping new contributors find relevant documentation
- Translating technical concepts into beginner-friendly language
- Identifying related contribution records
- Surfacing unresolved research questions
- Supporting maintainer handoffs

## Constraints

AI assistance must remain bounded by evidence. It should not invent authority, rewrite history, or produce unsupported protocol claims.

Key constraints:

- Source-linked answers
- Uncertainty labeling
- Human review for important decisions
- No automatic governance authority
- No hidden scoring of contributors
- Privacy-aware data access

## Mentor, Not Oracle

The AI Mentor Layer should behave as a guide through archived knowledge, not as an unquestionable decision-maker. It may help contributors learn faster, but final interpretation should remain with humans and governance processes.

## Design Position

AI can help ecosystems remember, teach, and navigate complexity. It should not become a centralized authority over reputation, contribution value, or historical truth.
